# Law Firm Digital Twin

Law Firm Digital Twin is the working repository for the AI-first insurance defense law firm simulation.

Initial scope:

- Build a synthetic, public-data-first simulation of an insurance defense firm.
- Start with labor and employment defense.
- Model the end-to-end lifecycle from intake, conflicts, carrier guidelines, billing, discovery, depositions, mediation, settlement, trial, and post-resolution finance.
- Keep real client data and unclear reuse rights out of the repo until explicit release gates exist.

Current status: the deterministic G0-G2 employment-defense walking skeleton, provider-neutral scaling contracts, projection/staged-artifact pipeline, executable artifact-specialist mesh, and qualified persona-aware channel renderer/corpus evaluators are a candidate after a 153-test full-suite baseline plus 19 passing focused dossier tests and 4 passing technology-adoption tests. Eight additional insurance-defense families now have source-empty, learning-ineligible, runtime-disabled design packs covering 80 evidence families, 31 expert disciplines, 62 persona roles, and 37 organization contexts; employment remains the only active family. A separate qualified, non-rendering employment matter-dossier catalog now maps 16 lifecycle stages and 176 document types; all resolution branches are inactive until a separately qualified kernel-event adapter exists, and the public fixture is aggregate-only. The architecture also adopts bounded future adapters for SimPy, Temporal, EDRM, Tika, Tesseract, SALI LMSS, LEDES, UTBMS, Synthea, FHIR, and specialized graph/domain components; no dependency is installed or runtime-authorized yet. Every active employment artifact is bound to a bounded planner, evidence-family writer, deterministic renderer, and four independent checker contracts; the mesh explicitly records that no runtime AI specialist executed. Public composition requires a fully revalidated case qualification and exact projection reselection. The parallel fixture has 90 unique G2 text bodies across ten cases, a merits-blind presentation scope, persistent-firm versus case-local identity rules, bounded organization-culture and individual-style projections, and separate integrity, diversity, and specialist-coverage reports; see docs/g2_build_status.md, docs/g2_scale_architecture.md, and docs/decisions/g2_artifact_specialist_mesh_decision.md. Persistent-author concentration and near-template warnings remain visible, so these are synthetic presentation controls rather than claims of human realism. The long-run campaign is specification-only. Jurisdiction rules remain DATA_FIRST_PENDING, and G3+, external data, runtime LLM specialists, native-document fidelity, unattended execution, and production use remain unauthorized.
