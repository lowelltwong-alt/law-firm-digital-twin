# Jurisdiction Data Rubric

H-1 is delegated through a data-first rubric. Public digital data availability is the highest-weighted factor.

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Public docket/document availability, including PACER/RECAP/CourtListener practicality | 30 |
| Insurance-defense / employment-defense case volume | 20 |
| Rule, form, standing-order, ECF, ADR, and local-practice availability | 15 |
| Outcome/procedure observability | 15 |
| State-law overlay usefulness for labor and employment defense | 10 |
| Cost/friction of acquisition and parsing | 5 |
| Future-firm relevance | 5 |

## Candidate Evidence Classes

- PACER and official U.S. Courts access information.
- RECAP/CourtListener coverage and document availability.
- Federal Judicial Center Integrated Database case-level metadata.
- EEOC enforcement and litigation datasets.
- Court local rules, standing orders, civil forms, ADR rules, and ECF guidance.
- Public opinions, orders, jury instructions, and statistics.

## Current State

No jurisdiction is locked. The first implementation uses a placeholder `DATA_FIRST_PENDING` rule pack that refuses to act as a real deadline authority until a scored jurisdiction record is admitted.
