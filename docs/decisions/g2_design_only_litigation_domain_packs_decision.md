# G2 Design-Only Litigation Domain Packs

Status: implemented as non-executable blueprints. Employment defense remains the only active G2 case family.

## Decision

Future litigation expertise is represented by a separate `DesignOnlyLitigationDomainPack` contract. It does not inherit from, convert to, or satisfy the executable `EvidenceDomainPack` accepted by the artifact-specialist mesh. A design pack has no source admission, runtime execution, learning eligibility, canonical-truth authority, activation receipt, or external access.

Eight design-only families are currently cataloged:

- medical malpractice;
- construction defect;
- premises and slip-fall liability;
- motor-vehicle liability;
- trucking and transportation;
- products liability;
- insurance coverage and extra-contractual defense; and
- software and technology professional liability.

Together they define 80 evidence-artifact families, 31 bounded expert disciplines, 62 persona roles, 37 organization contexts, and 80 lifecycle-stage contracts. These are requirements and risk models, not generated case files or claims of domain correctness.

## Evidence design

Each artifact family declares its business or system origin, simulated channel/native target, author and recipient roles, fact-domain scope, lifecycle range, metadata, lineage policy, custody risks, version risks, causal-noise siblings, allowed synthetic source classes, and prohibited content. Examples include clinical note/addendum timing, construction drawing and RFI revision chains, surveillance retention gaps, telematics clock drift, product exemplar custody, policy-form precedence, and software ticket-to-deployment linkage.

## Expert boundary

Expert disciplines declare only future source scope, method categories, mandatory limitations, independence checks, escalation conditions, and forbidden conclusions. They cannot claim credentials, execution, diagnosis, causation, credibility, compliance, legal conclusions, or real-world prediction. Every future activation requires protected discipline-specific fixtures and a separate human decision.

## Persona and organization boundary

Roles select controlled mechanisms: observation opportunity, encoding conditions, elapsed time, intervening information, retrieval confidence, correction history, audience, channel, thread context, review, workload, staffing, record access, approvals, handoffs, and competing priorities. MBTI, left/right-brain ideas, protected attributes, education, class, profession, disability, or language cannot determine grammar, intelligence, competence, credibility, liability, or outcome.

Organization contexts model systems, workflows, escalation paths, retention/versioning, silos, resource constraints, and formal-policy-versus-observed-practice differences. They do not assign personality or demographic traits to people.

## Activation and learning

Every pack is fixed to:

- `status=design_only`;
- `source_admission_state=no_sources_admitted`;
- `learning_state=not_eligible`;
- `runtime_execution=false`;
- `external_source_access=false`;
- `canonical_truth_write=false`; and
- `activation_gate=human_case_family_activation_and_domain_fixture_qualification`.

String receipts cannot bypass these literals. Activating one family would require a separate reviewed revision, source decisions, artifact-by-artifact protected fixtures, knowledge/authority/custody replay, sealed-label noninterference, privacy tests, expert-method qualification, and explicit Lowell approval.

## Public surface

The generated catalog publishes only pack/family identities and aggregate counts by artifact intent. It excludes artifact definitions, expert material lists, persona roles and states, fact domains, authors, recipients, prompts, source rows, credentials, case instances, and document bodies.
