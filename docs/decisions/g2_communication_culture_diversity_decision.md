# G2 Communication Culture and Diversity Decision

Status: selected for additive implementation on 2026-07-18.

## Problem

The qualified persona-channel fixture has 90 unique bodies, but most channels
have one role-level voice signature and one structural fingerprint. Opening and
closing variants create lexical uniqueness without proving meaningful author,
organization, or channel diversity.

## Decision

Add two provider-neutral, deterministic, presentation-only projections to the
qualified composition path:

1. an `OrganizationCultureProjection` describing synthetic operational
   conventions for recordkeeping, coordination, review, correction visibility,
   and channel layout; and
2. an `IndividualStyleProjection` describing a stable, matter-scoped writer
   pattern selected from reviewed, visibly realized presentation controls.

Neither projection is world truth, a demographic description, a personality
diagnosis, a credibility signal, or an outcome cause. They are rederived from a
fully qualified renderer projection. A caller cannot supply them to the public
renderer.

The base compiler, world kernel, Berean path, deterministic baseline renderer,
and `generated/g2-scale-v1` remain unchanged.

## Permitted inputs

Derivation may use only:

- a compiler-bound presentation-scope commitment derived before axis selection
  from synthetic design identity, ordinal, case family, and compiler revision;
- synthetic organization and actor identifiers;
- canonical explicit voice and memory controls from the renderer persona view;
- a versioned persistent-organization identity policy (currently the synthetic
  defense firm only); and
- a separate safe presentation episode for realized per-matter geometry;
- channel/capability contract;
- recipient count and channel compatibility;
- workload/stress as bounded layout pressure;
- lineage revision for visible correction presentation; and
- versioned culture/style catalogs.

Selection must not use world or matter namespaces because they commit sealed
axis labels. It also must not use assertion values, case strength, evidence direction,
procedural or representation quality, resolution path, conflict purpose,
evaluator labels, outcomes, raw background, training, goals, education, class,
profession, protected traits, MBTI, left/right-brain claims, diagnoses, or
unadmitted source material.

## Permitted effects

Culture and writer style may alter only:

- safe opening family;
- placement of nonfactual opening relative to record fields;
- field grouping and label geometry;
- section-heading geometry;
- connective and closing form;
- correction/revision visibility;
- compact versus expanded presentation; and
- allowlisted nonfactual blocks.

They may not create, remove, soften, strengthen, reorder, reconcile, or mutate a
factual assertion. Every assertion remains the canonical exact renderer output
and appears exactly once.

## Qualification boundary

The compiler creates a presentation-scope commitment from safe pre-axis inputs
and binds the source blueprint plus derived scope into `CaseCompilation`, its
compilation hash, and its qualification receipt. Validation recomputes the scope
from that admitted source and rejects an attacker-controlled replacement. The
public renderer continues to require the full compilation,
recomputed receipt, and an exact projection index. Culture and style projections
are derived after that validation from the safe presentation scope and the
reselected projection, then bound into the composition plan, candidate,
qualified bundle, and local receipts.

The explicitly named unqualified renderer may derive the same projections for
contract tests, but its output cannot enter the corpus builder.

## Diversity evaluation

Keep integrity/continuity checks in `corpus_auditor.py`. Add an independent
`corpus_diversity_evaluator.py` that operates on process-local structural
features from qualified bundles.

The public evaluator accepts only qualified compilation/receipt/bundle inputs
and revalidates each batch. Raw feature rows are private test inputs and cannot
self-attest qualified binding. It reports:

- factual-trace and qualification binding coverage;
- exact duplicate and near-template candidates;
- trivial-variant ratio;
- cross-channel schema separation;
- within-channel diversity from operations with manifested render effects;
- within-author continuity;
- cross-author collapse or caricature;
- organization-culture coverage/coherence only when eligible; and
- protected-proxy metamorphic results.

A stable persistent actor controls voice across matters. A separate safe
presentation episode may vary only realized geometry; it is never an actor or
organization identity and cannot hide continuity failures. A changed body hash,
opaque instance hash, world/matter namespace, or variant
never counts by itself as meaningful diversity. Declared controls without a
rendered effect are excluded from effective-family and presentation metrics.
Underpowered groups report `coverage_insufficient`, never a synthetic pass.

Public summaries contain aggregate channel metrics only for cells of at least
five artifacts. They exclude bodies, shingles, raw feature tags, person,
organization, matter, culture, assertion, candidate, and pair identities.

## Initial G2 gates

For each channel with at least ten artifacts:

- at least three effective voice or geometry families;
- no effective family above 70 percent concentration;
- meaningful presentation diversity ratio at least 0.20;
- exact assertion coverage and qualified binding equal 1.0;
- exact-body clone count zero; and
- trivial lexical variation is reported separately and cannot satisfy the
  diversity gate.

These are synthetic fixture gates, not human-realism, cultural-validity, legal,
native-format, or production-fidelity claims.

## Stops and rollback

Stop and roll back the culture/style adapter if any change:

- affects assertion bytes, coverage, trace, order, source, or conflict presence;
- responds to world/matter namespaces, sealed axes, raw background/training/goals,
  role label alone, a protected or
  demographic proxy, merits, outcome, or evaluator data;
- crosses world/matter/actor scope;
- accepts a forged culture/style object at the qualified boundary;
- creates grammar degradation, credibility, competence, diligence, liability,
  or outcome implications;
- leaks process-local culture/style identities or features publicly; or
- reduces diversity to punctuation, whitespace, synonyms, or opener variants.

Rollback removes the additive culture/style derivation and diversity evaluator
and restores the current qualified persona-channel renderer. No base compiler
or world rollback is required.

## Invalidation

Requalify on changes to culture/style catalogs or schemas, safe persona controls,
channel contracts, lexicon, geometry planner, qualified boundary, assertion
renderer, feature extractor, diversity policy, privacy surface, or source
fixture.
