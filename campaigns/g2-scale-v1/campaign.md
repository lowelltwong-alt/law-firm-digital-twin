# G2 Scale Qualification Campaign

Campaign ID: `law-firm-digital-twin-g2-scale-v1`  
Revision: `1`  
Mode: `specification_only`

## Observable objective

Qualify the provider-neutral contracts, least-privilege projections, validators, specialist mesh, and Cursor runtime-adapter specification required for exactly ten varied synthetic employment-defense matters without crossing G2.

This campaign is the qualification pilot for later hundreds- or thousands-of-case work. It does not authorize that later scale.

## What good looks like

- Existing G2 behavior remains green and unrelated user work is untouched.
- Only the world kernel owns canonical facts, authority, custody, and outcomes.
- Exactly ten blueprints cover varied merits, evidence shape, procedural quality, representation quality, and resolution tracks.
- Operating and renderer projections cannot access sealed truth, design labels, or join keys.
- Every artifact plan names a qualified evidence capability, fact allowlist, knowledge frontier, version lineage, and custody origin.
- Replay from locked seed and manifest produces identical canonical hashes.
- Voice and representation checks reject template collapse, caricature, and deterministic stereotype proxies.
- All leases, attempts, budgets, receipts, and stop decisions reconcile.
- No external data, real identity, legal-authority claim, G3 rendering, publication, or deployment occurs.

## Controller and worker authority

The controller alone owns `.campaign-state/g2-scale-v1/`, leases, state transitions, receipt append, budget accounting, and auto-advance. Workers may submit evidence but may never edit controller state.

Every worker receives exactly one job contract. It may read declared inputs and write declared outputs only. A worker cannot change the campaign, validators, gold standards, budgets, or its own gate.

## Auto-advance

A job advances only when:

1. every dependency passed at its expected digest;
2. an uncontested lease is active;
3. input and dependency digests match;
4. every required gate returns `pass`;
5. output, receipt, and checkpoint are durable;
6. the attempt, storage, context, time, and cost budgets remain;
7. no mandatory stop or human gate is active; and
8. the next job has a disjoint write scope.

Only `pass` advances. `inconclusive` never means pass.

## Attempts and repair

Each job receives one original attempt and at most one bounded repair. A changed model, adapter, validator, schema, source, authority, privacy boundary, or scope creates a new revision?not a silent retry. An unchanged deterministic failure is never retried.

Transient failures receive at most one retry after 600 seconds. Repair preserves the first failure receipt and reruns the failed gate plus affected regressions.

## Hard ceilings

- 72 wall-clock hours;
- 100 abstract cost units;
- 20 total attempts;
- 3 parallel jobs;
- 250 MB total storage;
- 24,000 context tokens per job;
- 90 minutes per job; and
- 20 MB output per job.

Reaching a ceiling stops before the next effect. It never relaxes a gate or establishes completion.

## Human gates

Human approval is required for the baseline digest and budget, unattended launch, any active case family beyond employment, changes to qualified schemas or validators, external data, G3, budget extensions, and publication or deployment.

At revision 1, both J000 and J410 are blocking human jobs. No launch receipt exists.

## Mandatory stops

The controller must stop, preserve non-sensitive evidence, release leases, and report the exact resume precondition for: user interrupt, budget exhaustion, authority boundary, pending human gate, repeated failure, gold-standard failure, security/privacy signal, stale dependency, write conflict, or inconclusive validation.

Real/client/private-data indicators, attempted network access, truth leakage, or a request to weaken a protected gate are terminal for the current attempt.
